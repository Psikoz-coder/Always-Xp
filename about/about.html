About Windows XP

Released by Microsoft in 2001, Windows XP revolutionized the computing world with its user-friendly interface and stability.
That iconic "Bliss" wallpaper, with its rolling green hills and bright blue sky, became an unforgettable part of tech history.
But XP wasn’t just about looks! Here’s why it became a legend:

The Start Menu

Windows XP’s Start menu set the standard for modern operating systems. Everything was just a click away!


The Blue Screen of Death (BSOD)

XP was stable, but if something went wrong, you were greeted with the infamous blue screen. It was a rite of passage!


Internet Explorer 6

A legendary browser… mostly for its bugs and security issues!


Theme Support

Loved the classic Windows 98 look? Or preferred the sleek Luna theme? XP gave you the power to personalize.


Music & Games

Manage your tunes with Windows Media Player 9 or lose hours playing classics like Minesweeper and FreeCell.


Support for Windows XP officially ended in 2014, but for many nostalgic users, it remains unforgettable. If you still love XP, you’re a true tech historian!
